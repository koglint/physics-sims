Formula Derivation

Ideal Banked Curve Speed

Reveal the derivation of v = √(r g tan θ) one step at a time.

Back to simulation
Step 1

Resolve the normal force into components on the banked road.

Resolve FN into a right triangle.

The components use the same vertical and horizontal directions as the road-view diagram.

Step 2

FNy = m g

Vertical balance

FNy balances mg.

Step 3

FNx = m v2 / r

Horizontal balance

FNx provides the centripetal force mv2/r.

Step 4

FNx = FN sin θ and FNy = FN cos θ

Match each component to the triangle

Use the geometry of the force triangle to rewrite the component equations in trig form.

Step 5

(FNx / FNy) = (FN sin θ / FN cos θ) = (m v2 / r) / (m g)

Divide the equations

The horizontal-to-vertical component ratio now lines up directly with a trig ratio.

Step 6

tan θ = v2 / (r g)

Simplify

FN and m cancel, leaving tan θ.

Step 7

v2 = r g tan θ

Rearrange for v2

Multiply both sides by rg.

Step 8

v = √(r g tan θ)

Take the square root

This gives the final expression v = √(rg tan θ).
